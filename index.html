SITE UPDATE [28-01-2026 - whilst on annual leave]

Status Update: Rural Life & Computer Vision

At least this update didn't take as long as the previous one... After finally settling 150 miles (ish) away, I've been working on streamlining the computer vision analysis process. Common problems can be found irrespective of the context or target outcome for a vision task, and I am aiming to resolve those problems.

I have created a repo called Plum Process, so named after the first person to reach out to me when I had my car stuck in a bog (living in the rural countryside can be interesting). I've also been working on a necessary extension of the COCO JSON standard , the rough workings of which can be found in my PlumProcess repo.

These aforementioned implementations have been worked on in conjunction with my day-to-day tasks as a Data Scientist at Cefas (the benefits of youth and neurodiversity induced time blindness).

I should also be posting the progress I made on 150k, the travel app I work on and off on. The app itself was being built to experiment with graph networks and causal analysis . Flowcharts and rough algorithms were developed a while ago, but development stalls for months as it becomes a bit of a mountain to develop by yourself, whilst working on complex end-to-end computer vision models, researching out-of-the-box solutions for several scientific projects, and being a city-raised clutz.

I do aim to publish more here, but it'll most likely be in the form of my handwritten notes from my Remarkable. As always, feel free to shoot me a message.

I’m a Data Scientist and AI/ML Researcher focused on building practical computer vision pipelines and scalable algorithms grounded in reality. For me, impactful engineering isn't about chasing theoretical perfection with unlimited compute; it's about bounding complex spatial problems by real-world constraints, openly challenging standard methodologies when they fall short, and knowing when to step back from the terminal to gain strategic clarity.

Core Focus & Technical Deep Dives

  • Engineering Reality Over Theory: My foundational work stems from an ongoing evolution in how machines see the world—documented in my journey into computer vision—which directly drives how I tackle high-performance bottlenecks like GPU-accelerated image shape analysis.
  • Questioning the Defaults: I constantly look to interrogate standard approaches to see if they actually serve the task at hand. Sometimes that means building specialized workarounds like the SAHI polygon splitter, deciding exactly how I ditched IoUs for more robust custom metrics, or choosing to fundamentally rethink human pose estimation.
  • Reframing the Problem: When edge cases hit hard, I rely on mental models that flip the perspective. Applying the inverted problem helps strip away noise to isolate core algorithmic truths, while drawing cross-domain inspiration from unexpected places—like NLP reflections by the Bosporus Strait—keeps pipelines adaptable.

Philosophy & Mindset

  • Strategic Deliberation: Good code requires quiet thought. My personal approach to research prioritizes methodical execution, which often starts with the conscious decision to let go of the keyboard to map out architecture before writing a single line.
  • Introspection & Autonomy: I believe sustainable research requires room for unstructured, candid reflection—whether capturing raw thoughts on a quiet Sunday, July something, examining the internal resistance of why fear?, or exploring what true creative and technical freedom is.
  • Community & Collaboration: None of this work happens in a vacuum. Beyond the codebases, I view building robust data ecosystems as a collective effort, offering a rallying call to foster open, collaborative problem-solving across the industry.
